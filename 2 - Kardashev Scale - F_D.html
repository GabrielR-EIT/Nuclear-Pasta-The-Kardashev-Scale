Fermi & Drake

In a nutshell, the ‘Fermi Paradox’, which itself is a misnomer as it isn’t even technically a paradox, describes the apparent ‘waste of space’ when it seems that the universe should more likely be festering with life.

Here is where the ‘Drake Equation’ enters the situation.

If you count the number of planets in our galaxy that have all the right conditions for life to thrive, you find that, even with pessimistic yet informed estimations, there should be anywhere between 1,000 and 100,000,000 civilizations in our galaxy alone.

We are made of hydrogen, carbon, calcium, iron, oxygen, and a number of other elements that are found in abundance out there in space.

If the ingredients of life and the tools to make it aren't all that rare, then what makes life itself so rare?
