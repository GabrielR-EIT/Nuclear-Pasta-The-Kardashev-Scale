Introduction

In 1964, a Soviet astronomer by the name of Nikolai Kardashev coined the idea of a measurement system that categorizes a civilization based on its energy consumption. The more energy they use, the higher up the Kardashev Scale they will be. For comparison, humans are currently at roughly 0.73 on the scale.

In a nutshell, these are the basic milestones for any advanced civilization:

Type 1: Harnessing all available energy of the host planet

Type 2: Harnessing all available energy of the host solar system

Type 3: Harnessing all available energy of the host galaxy

Each step is comparable to humans first harnessing fire, giving rise to, at the time, unimaginable advancements.

Michio Kaku estimates that, assuming a 3% per year increase in humanity’s total energy consumption (oil, solar, wind, geothermal, etc.), it would take 100 – 200 years to reach Type 1 status, Type 2 may take a few thousand years, and Type 3 status could be attained in roughly one million years.

We are tantalisingly close to Type 1.

There are people alive today that, considering today’s medical breakthroughs and assuming the average person born today could potentially live for 150+ years, could see us make the leap to the very first rung on this incredible ladder.
